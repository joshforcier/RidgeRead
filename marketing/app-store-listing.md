### Description

**Read terrain like a guide. Scout any unit from your couch.**

RidgeRead analyzes real terrain data to show you where elk are feeding, bedding, wallowing, and traveling — specific to the season and time of day you're hunting.

This isn't another topo map with waypoints. RidgeRead pulls actual USGS elevation data, computes slope and aspect for every feature in your area, maps real land cover from satellite data (streams, meadows, timber stands, roads), then uses AI to identify the specific terrain features that hold elk.

**HOW IT WORKS**
1. Drop a 2-mile box on any terrain in elk country
2. Select your season (Rut, Post-Rut, Late Season) and time of day
3. Tap Analyze — real terrain data is pulled and scored in seconds
4. Get AI-generated points of interest placed on real terrain features, each with tactical hunting advice

**WHAT MAKES IT DIFFERENT**

Real data, not guesswork:
- 400-point elevation grid from USGS for every analysis
- Slope, aspect, and relief computed from actual measurements
- Saddles, benches, ridgelines, drainages, and finger ridges detected automatically
- Every road, trail, stream, meadow, and forest mapped from OpenStreetMap
- Customizable road/trail buffer (0.1–2 miles) — no POIs near human activity

Season-specific elk behavior:
- Rut: Wallows peak. Bulls on north-facing benches at 10-30° slope within 400m of water. Travel along saddles and ridgelines at dawn/dusk.
- Post-Rut: Bulls go silent, hide in densest north-facing timber (80%+ canopy, 20-40° slopes). May not leave timber for days.
- Late Season: Aspect flips to south-facing. Large herds on predictable patterns between south-facing meadows and south-facing bedding timber. Most patternable elk of the year.

**FEATURES**
- 4 base maps: Topo, Satellite, Street, Terrain
- 5 behavior layers: Feeding, Water, Bedding, Wallows, Travel Corridors
- Season and time-of-day controls that recalculate everything instantly
- Behavior weight percentages show which activities dominate each time window
- Key zone overlays with color-coded behavior circles
- Tactical POI descriptions referencing actual elevation, slope, and aspect
- Works anywhere in North America — no pre-built unit maps required

**BUILT BY ELK HUNTERS**
We got tired of staring at topo lines and guessing. RidgeRead reads terrain the way an experienced guide reads terrain — it just does it in 10 seconds for any drainage in the West.

Every POI comes with tactical hunting advice referencing the real elevation, slope angle, and aspect at that spot. No generic tips. No guesswork.

SEASON-SPECIFIC BEHAVIOR MODELING
Elk on October 1st are not the same elk on November 15th. RidgeRead models three phases:

Rut (Sep-Oct): Breeding mode. Bulls bugle from saddles, bed on north-facing benches at 10-30 degrees within 400m of water. Wallows are critical — flat boggy areas at drainage heads in timber. Travel peaks at dawn and dusk.

Post-Rut (Oct-Nov): Recovery. Bulls have lost 15-20% body weight. They go silent and hide in the densest north-facing timber available — 80%+ canopy, deadfall, steep slopes. They may not leave timber for days. The hardest phase to hunt.

Late Season (Nov-Dec): Survival. Aspect flips — elk move to south and southwest-facing slopes for solar warmth (15-25 degrees F warmer). Large herds, predictable daily patterns. South-facing transition zones between feeding meadows and bedding timber are the #1 feature.

Toggle season and time of day — every POI, overlay, and weight recalculates instantly.

- Computed slope, aspect, and terrain feature detection
